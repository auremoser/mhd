Divination + Data Journalism


"You should not work for a non-profit, when the for-pro[phe]ts can predict the future"

And there is something to be said, as comical as that sounds, for the idea that journalist developers are expected to divine the future with data. In many ways I have a ritual and practice that I use in approach to my work, and so I'm going to go over some of that today

"Developer-Journalist"


Technologist working with data and news apps development.

Developer working on visual representations of abstract info to reinforce human understanding.

Data Journalist communicating quantitative information with qualitative nuance.

Etc...

Goal of News Applications

Adopt a triangle methodology:
simply communicate data fact and avoid narrative fiction

Goal of News Applications

Pyramids To Predict Success


I think often, our process aligns with this pyramid for greater information. This kind o simple pictographic iconography is the kind of intuitive legibility we like to infuse into our applications. With an emphasis on preparation, design planning and data cleaning, you can end up with a visual that is more informative than forboding, something that builds the foundation for a good narrative, and fractalizes throughout a story that is greater than its development parts.

Goal of News Applications

with minimum visual complexity

Toolbox

  • Backend: Python/RVM + PHP
  • Code Storage: Git + Phabricator
  • Asset Storage: Dropbox
  • Comms: IRC/Skype Chat
  • Data Cleaning: Open Refine
  • Frontend: JS (D3, Vega, Highcharts, Miso)

Workflow

  • Define roles
  • Data gathering
  • Data processing
  • Data practice
  • Data visualization + development


The refrain from all of this is that thorough understanding of your audience, your data and your dev team resources is essential for each project. and really defines its constraints and success potential.

Summary

  • Consider your approach + audience
  • Define your constraints
  • Select application/project type
  • Distribute your resources
  • Build + test

Who is your audience? What is your data + objective What types of visualization would you like to create? What are the logical steps to creating them?
